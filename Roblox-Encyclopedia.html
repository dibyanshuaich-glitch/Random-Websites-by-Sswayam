// Origin Story

The History of Roblox

From a physics sandbox built in a garage to a platform reshaping how an entire generation plays, creates, and earns.

2003
The Idea Is Born
David Baszucki, fresh from selling his physics simulation company, begins sketching a multiplayer physics sandbox with Erik Cassel. Inspired by watching users joyfully crash blocks and collapse bridges in "Interactive Physics," he imagines something far bigger: a world where players aren't consumers ‚Äî they're creators. Development begins.
SPARK OF GENIUS
2004
DynaBlocks ‚Äî The First Prototype
The platform is first coded as DynaBlocks. Internal testing begins. The engine lets blocks collide, stack, and react to gravity. The pair are convinced that letting users build rather than just play is the future. Rough servers, temperamental physics, but the dream is crystal clear.
PROTOTYPE
2005
Renamed ROBLOX ‚Äî Alpha Launch
DynaBlocks is rebranded as Roblox ‚Äî fusing "robots" and "blocks." The alpha goes live with a tiny tester group. User #1 is the official Roblox account. User #2 becomes the legendary "John Doe." User #3 is "Jane Doe." The mythology begins.
ALPHA LAUNCH
2006
Public Beta ‚Äî September 1st
Roblox opens to the public on September 1, 2006. Roblox Studio debuts with Lua scripting ‚Äî transforming Roblox from a game into a platform. Early iconic creations emerge: obstacle courses, sword arenas, roleplay worlds. Players share links on forums and school computers. The community ignites.
PUBLIC LAUNCH
2007
Robux & Tix ‚Äî The Dual Economy
Roblox introduces Robux (paid currency) and Tix (free daily login currency). Players can buy hats, gear, and accessories. Social status is established through catalog ownership. The first virtual millionaires emerge among top game developers.
ECONOMY LAUNCH
2010
1 Million Users Milestone
Roblox crosses 1 million registered users. Forums buzz with clan wars and game reviews. Famous early games: Natural Disaster Survival, Sword Fight on the Heights, Work at a Pizza Place, Flood Escape. The first YouTube channels emerge recording Roblox gameplay.
1M USERS
2012
iOS & Android ‚Äî Mobile Invasion
Roblox launches on iOS and Android. The blocky aesthetic ‚Äî once a limitation ‚Äî is a mobile advantage. Developer Exchange (DevEx) launches, allowing creators to convert Robux to real USD. Teenage developers start earning thousands monthly. Roblox becomes more than a hobby.
MOBILE LAUNCH
2013
Erik Cassel Passes Away
On February 11, 2013, co-founder Erik Cassel passes away after a three-year battle with cancer. The community holds in-game vigils. Baszucki wrote: "Erik was my co-founder, my partner, and my friend." His engineering legacy lives in every block of the platform.
IN MEMORIAM
2016
Tix Removed. Xbox & VR Arrive.
Roblox removes Tix on April 14, 2016 ‚Äî one of its most controversial decisions. Forums erupt. Simultaneously, Roblox launches on Xbox One and adds VR support. YouTubers like Flamingo, Denis, and The Pals explode in popularity, each gaining millions of subscribers.
TIX REMOVED ‚Ä¢ XBOX LAUNCH
2018
Jailbreak, Adopt Me & The Golden Era
Jailbreak by asimo3089 and badcc becomes the most played game on the platform. Adopt Me! begins its meteoric rise. Roblox invests heavily in its developer community through summits and grants. The platform matures from a kids' game into a creative economy.
GOLDEN ERA
2020
The COVID Explosion
Global pandemic locks the world indoors. Daily active users surge to 37 million. The Lil Nas X virtual concert draws 33M+ attendees. Roblox processes over a billion hours of gameplay monthly. Servers strain. Records shatter. Nothing is ever the same.
PANDEMIC EXPLOSION
2021
NYSE IPO ‚Äî $45 Billion Valuation
On March 10, 2021, Roblox goes public on the NYSE under RBLX. Opening valuation: $45.2 billion. Developers collectively earned $538 million through DevEx that year alone. Corporate America takes notice. Roblox is no longer just a kids' game.
NYSE:RBLX IPO
2023‚Äì2026
AI Tools, 17+ Content & Beyond
AI-powered creation tools launch ‚Äî users generate terrain, code, and items using natural language. A 17+ content rating opens the door to mature themes. Spatial voice chat, avatar upgrades, and brand partnerships multiply. In 2026, with 380M+ users, Roblox continues evolving at warp speed.

// The Mythology

Hackers & Legends

Some were myths born of imagination. Some were real threats. All became permanent parts of Roblox's folklore.

JOHN DOE
MYTH
User #2. A staff test account that sparked a 2017 viral panic: "John Doe will hack all accounts on March 18th." Schools banned Roblox. Parents called tech support. News articles ran. Complete fabrication ‚Äî but John Doe became Roblox's most iconic ghost forever.
USER #2 ‚Ä¢ 2017 PANIC
GUEST 666
CREEPYPASTA
A "demon guest" who appeared in your server, stood motionless, stared at your character, then crashed your game. Fan videos were shared millions of times. Screenshots were manipulated to "prove" his existence. Guests were real (unregistered players) but #666 was pure fiction ‚Äî terrifying to a 10-year-old at 2am.
1x1x1x1
BANNED
The most notorious alleged hacker account. Roblox confirmed and terminated it. Whether 1x1x1x1 had real server-level exploit capabilities or was mythologized remains debated. The name became synonymous with Roblox's dark side and spawned novels, ARGs, and short films.
CONFIRMED BANNED
LILLY_S
MYSTERY
One of Roblox's most authentic mysteries. An all-black avatar appearing in games during 2013‚Äì2014, standing still and watching before vanishing. Unlike most myths, genuine independent screenshots exist. Roblox never officially explained the account. One of the few legends that might actually be real.
JANE DOE
MYTH
User #3. The female counterpart to John Doe ‚Äî also a staff test account. Often paired with John Doe in myths, described as a "couple" haunting players together. Both became cultural shorthand for "mysterious Roblox figure," inspiring thousands of fan stories and animated videos.
REAL HACKERS
DOCUMENTED
Beyond myths, real threats exist. A 2020 exploit exposed inappropriate content on a child's account in a widely-reported incident. In 2023, internal Roblox DevCon data was breached. Robux scams, phishing sites, and script injection attacks cost players millions annually. Roblox employs hundreds in Trust & Safety to fight back.
