⛏ WHAT IS MINECRAFT?

🌍

Infinite Open World

Minecraft drops players into a procedurally generated world with virtually infinite terrain made up of three-dimensional blocks. Mountains, forests, caves, oceans, and deserts all await exploration — no two worlds are ever the same.

SANDBOX GENRE
⚒️

Craft & Survive

Players gather raw materials, craft tools, build shelters, and fend off hostile creatures like Creepers, Zombies, and Skeletons. The game's crafting system lets you combine items on a crafting table to create entirely new ones.

SURVIVAL MODE
🏗️

Unlimited Creativity

Creative Mode hands players unlimited resources and the ability to fly, letting them construct anything imaginable — from simple houses to massive cities, working computers, and even entire fictional universes recreated in block form.

CREATIVE MODE
🔴

Redstone Engineering

Redstone is Minecraft's version of electrical circuits. It allows players to build incredibly complex machines, automations, contraptions, and even functional computers within the game — a testament to player creativity.

REDSTONE
🌐

Multiplayer & Community

Minecraft supports multiplayer servers where thousands of players can coexist. Its massive community has created countless mods, texture packs, custom maps, and servers — extending the game's life indefinitely.

MULTIPLAYER
📱

Cross-Platform

Minecraft is available on virtually every platform — PC (Java & Bedrock), Xbox, PlayStation, Nintendo Switch, iOS, Android, and even VR. The Bedrock Edition allows cross-platform play between all non-Java platforms.

MULTI-PLATFORM

📜 HISTORY OF MINECRAFT

The Birth of Minecraft

Markus "Notch" Persson, a Swedish programmer at jAlbum, creates the first version of Minecraft — originally called "Cave Game" — over a single weekend in early May 2009. Inspired by Infiniminer and Dwarf Fortress, Notch releases the first public alpha (Classic) to TIGSource Forums on May 17, 2009. The game gains an enthusiastic following almost immediately, selling 7 copies a day at first.

📅 MAY 17, 2009 — PUBLIC ALPHA

Survival & Indev Phase

In October 2009, a skeleton of Survival Mode is added to Classic — including enemies like Spiders, as well as TNT blocks. In December 2009, Minecraft enters the "Indev" (In Development) phase, introducing paintings by artist Kristoffer Zetterstrand. Notch quits his day job to focus on Minecraft full-time.

🧪 SURVIVAL TEST BEGINS

Alpha & Redstone

On June 30, 2010, Minecraft enters the Alpha phase. Redstone — a revolutionary material that transmits electrical signals — is introduced. Biomes and the Nether dimension are added in Alpha v1.2.0. Notch refuses a job offer at Valve Corporation to continue working on Minecraft. The crafting system is also introduced this year.

⚡ REDSTONE INTRODUCED

Mojang Founded & Beta

Notch officially founds Mojang Studios with co-founders Carl Manneh (CEO) and Jakob Porser. On December 20, 2010, Minecraft moves into its Beta phase. Notch sets up the studio with skilled programmers. Beta 1.0 introduces throwable eggs and leaf decay.

🏢 MOJANG STUDIOS FOUNDED

The Adventure Update & Full Release

Minecraft 1.0 is officially released on November 18, 2011 at MineCon — Mojang's own convention. The Adventure Update (Beta 1.8) adds the Ender Dragon boss, new biomes, sprinting, critical hits, and a hunger system. Mojang sells 4 million copies by release date. Jens "Jeb" Bergensten takes over as lead developer.

🎉 MINECRAFT 1.0 RELEASED

Console Debut

Minecraft launches on Xbox 360 on May 12, 2012 — developed by 4J Studios. It becomes one of the fastest-selling Xbox Live Arcade titles ever. The "Pretty Scary" update adds a host of new mobs including Witches, Bats, and Wither Skeletons. Mojang acquires CraftBukkit developers to improve server infrastructure.

🎮 XBOX 360 LAUNCH

PlayStation & Record Revenue

Minecraft launches on PlayStation 3. Mojang integrates Twitch streaming and releases a new launcher. The company records revenues of $330 million and profits of $129 million in this year alone. "The Update That Changed the World" brings massive improvements to world generation and biomes.

💰 $330M REVENUE

Microsoft Acquires Mojang

Microsoft acquires Mojang Studios — including all Minecraft intellectual property — for $2.5 billion. Notch suggested the deal himself on Twitter. The purchase brings Notch's net worth to $1.5 billion. He departs Mojang after the deal. The Minecraft community reacts with concern but Microsoft pledges to keep the game independent.

💼 $2.5B MICROSOFT DEAL

Bedrock Edition Unification

Minecraft: Pocket Edition (mobile) is fully released. The Windows 10 Edition — later renamed Bedrock Edition — reaches its full 1.0 release. Bedrock Edition becomes the unified cross-platform version of Minecraft allowing players on Xbox, Nintendo Switch, mobile, and Windows to play together seamlessly.

📱 BEDROCK ERA BEGINS

Minecraft Renaissance

Minecraft experiences a massive revival in popularity, largely driven by YouTube and streaming culture. Dream, PewDiePie, and other creators reignite global interest. Minecraft YouTube content surges past billions of views. The game becomes a cultural phenomenon for a new generation of players.

🔥 THE GREAT REVIVAL

Dream SMP & Content Golden Age

Dream and GeorgeNotFound create the Dream SMP — a private Minecraft server that becomes a global cultural phenomenon featuring scripted roleplay storylines involving dozens of top creators including Technoblade, TommyInnit, and Wilbur Soot. Minecraft content surpasses one trillion total YouTube views in 2021.

🎭 DREAM SMP ERA

Technoblade's Passing

Beloved YouTuber Technoblade (Alexander) passes away from sarcoma cancer on June 30, 2022, at age 23. His final video "So Long Nerds" is uploaded by his father and becomes one of the most-watched tribute videos in YouTube history with over 87 million views. The Minecraft launcher adds a crown to the pig in his honor. "Technoblade Never Dies" becomes a universal rallying cry.

💔 RIP TECHNOBLADE

300 Million & A Movie

Minecraft surpasses 300 million total sales, cementing its status as the best-selling video game of all time — surpassing Tetris. The game continues receiving major updates including Caves & Cliffs, The Wild Update, and Trails & Tails. A major Minecraft movie is announced and produced, premiering in 2025.

🏆 300M+ COPIES SOLD

👤 PROMINENT FIGURES

Markus Persson

aka "Notch"

The creator of Minecraft. Born in Stockholm, Sweden, Notch began programming at age seven on his father's Commodore 128. He developed the first version of Minecraft in just days in May 2009 while working at jAlbum. His vision of a limitless sandbox world changed gaming forever. He sold Mojang to Microsoft for $2.5 billion in 2014 and departed the company, citing mental exhaustion and public pressure.

CREATOR OF MINECRAFT

Jens Bergensten

aka "Jeb_"

The current lead developer of Minecraft, Jens took over creative control from Notch in December 2011. Under Jeb's leadership, Minecraft received countless landmark updates — including the Nether Update, Caves & Cliffs, and many new biomes, mobs, and mechanics. Known for his calm, player-focused development philosophy, Jeb has steered Minecraft for over a decade since Notch's departure.

LEAD DEVELOPER

Carl Manneh

CEO of Mojang (2010–2014)

Carl Manneh co-founded Mojang Studios with Notch and Jakob Porser in 2010, having previously worked with Notch at jAlbum. As CEO, Manneh led the business side of Mojang through its meteoric rise, orchestrating the landmark $2.5 billion acquisition by Microsoft in 2014. His business acumen was pivotal in turning a small indie studio into a global gaming empire.

CO-FOUNDER / CEO

Nathan Adams

aka "Dinnerbone"

One of Minecraft's most beloved developers, Dinnerbone joined Mojang in 2011. Known for his quirky contributions — including flipping his own in-game character upside-down as an Easter egg — Dinnerbone is responsible for many key Mojang technical systems, including the new launcher and major backend infrastructure improvements that made Minecraft scalable to millions of players.

CORE DEVELOPER

Daniel Rosenfeld

aka "C418"

The composer behind Minecraft's iconic, hauntingly beautiful soundtrack. C418 created the ambient music that defines the Minecraft experience — tracks like "Sweden," "Wet Hands," "Subwoofer Lullaby," and "Living Mice" are arguably the most recognized video game music in history. His ambient, minimalist style perfectly complements the game's open-ended creative atmosphere.

ORIGINAL COMPOSER

Jakob Porsér

Co-Founder of Mojang

Jakob Porsér co-founded Mojang alongside Notch and Carl Manneh. He worked with Notch previously and helped shape the early direction of the studio. Jakob also worked on Scrolls — Mojang's second game — which featured elements from board games and collectible card games. His entrepreneurial partnership with Notch was crucial in establishing Mojang as a real studio.

CO-FOUNDER

✝ RIP
Technoblade
~11M Subscribers · 2013–2022

Known as "The Blade," Technoblade (real name Alexander) was widely regarded as one of the greatest Minecraft PvP players of all time. He rose to fame through Hypixel tournaments and Minecraft Mondays, winning four times and defeating top creators. His rivalry with Dream culminated in a $100,000 MrBeast duel — which Technoblade won. He joined the Dream SMP in 2020 and created iconic storylines. In August 2021 he announced he had sarcoma cancer. He passed away on June 30, 2022. His final message: "Technoblade out." His last video became the #1 trending video on YouTube in 2022 with 87 million views.

🎮 GAME MODES

⚔️

Survival Mode

The classic Minecraft experience. Gather resources, build shelter, craft tools, grow food, and fight off hostile mobs. Manage your health, hunger, and oxygen as you explore and build in a world full of danger and wonder.

🏗️

Creative Mode

Unlimited resources, infinite health, and the ability to fly. Build anything your imagination can conceive — from modest homes to entire continents. The perfect mode for builders and architects.

🗺️

Adventure Mode

Designed for custom maps and player-created adventures. Block placement and destruction is restricted, encouraging players to engage with environments exactly as map creators intended. Perfect for story-driven experiences.

👁️

Spectator Mode

Fly invisibly through any block and observe the world without interacting. Perfect for builders scouting locations, reviewers assessing builds, or players wanting to experience other worlds without participation.

💀

Hardcore Mode

Survival Mode with one life only — permadeath. Once you die, the world is deleted. It's Minecraft at its most ruthless and tension-filled. Widely regarded as the ultimate Minecraft challenge by the community.

🌐

Multiplayer SMP

Survival Multiplayer — thousands of servers host communities of players coexisting, trading, building, and competing. From vanilla SMPs to highly modded servers, multiplayer extends Minecraft's lifespan infinitely.

🌌 THE THREE DIMENSIONS

🌍

The Overworld

The primary dimension and home dimension of Minecraft. A procedurally generated world featuring dozens of unique biomes — from lush forests and snowy mountains to scorching deserts and dark swamps. Nearly infinite in size, the Overworld is where players begin every adventure.

  • 60+ unique biomes
  • Deep Dark & Ancient Cities
  • Strongholds with End Portals
  • Villages, Temples, Monuments
  • Mineshafts & Dungeons
  • Caves & Cliffs system
  • Trial Chambers (1.21)
🔥

The Nether

A hellish, dangerous dimension accessed through obsidian Nether Portals. The Nether is filled with lava oceans, dangerous mobs, and ancient structures. Travelling in the Nether covers 8x the horizontal distance of the Overworld, making it essential for long-distance travel. It is home to some of Minecraft's rarest and most powerful materials.

  • Lava Oceans & Fire
  • Nether Fortresses
  • Bastions of the Piglins
  • Crimson & Warped Forests
  • Ghasts, Blazes, Wither Skeletons
  • Ancient Debris → Netherite
  • Soul Sand Valley
🌌

The End

A mysterious, void-filled dimension accessed through End Portals in Strongholds. The End is home to the Ender Dragon — Minecraft's final boss. After defeating the Dragon, players unlock the outer End islands, filled with End Cities, Chorus Fruit, and Shulkers. It is the official "ending" of Minecraft — though the game truly never ends.

  • Ender Dragon boss
  • Endermen everywhere
  • End Cities with Elytra
  • Chorus Fruit & Plants
  • Shulker Boxes origin
  • End Stone biome
  • Exit portal with poem

🌟 THE LEGACY OF MINECRAFT

A Game That Changed Everything

When Markus Persson released a janky, glitch-filled block game on a forum for indie developers in May 2009, nobody could have predicted what it would become. Minecraft didn't have a marketing budget, a publisher, a polished trailer, or even a proper name for the first few days. What it had was an idea so simple and so powerful that it couldn't help but spread — the idea that players should be free to do anything they wanted in a world they could shape entirely with their own hands.
Cultural Phenomenon

By 2021, Minecraft YouTube content had surpassed one trillion views — a number almost incomprehensible in scale. The game has been played by over 180 million people monthly. It has inspired novels, toys, television shows, clothing lines, theme park rides, and a Hollywood blockbuster. It is taught in schools worldwide through the Education Edition, helping children learn mathematics, history, environmental science, and computer programming. Minecraft has transcended the category of "video game" and become a medium unto itself.
"Minecraft is arguably the most important video game ever created.
Not the most technically impressive, not the most cinematic — the most important.
It gave a generation of children unlimited creative power and said:
'Go build something.'"
The Community That Built a World

The Minecraft community is one of the largest and most creative in gaming history. Players have constructed 1:1 scale replicas of Earth, fully functional computers using Redstone, pixel art canvases of historic paintings, and entire civilizations with working economies and governments. The Dream SMP — a scripted roleplay Minecraft server — attracted a global audience in 2020 and became what Wired magazine described as "a form of live theatre."
Technoblade Never Dies

The passing of Technoblade in June 2022 united the Minecraft community in grief and celebration of a life well lived. His final video garnered 87 million views, becoming one of the most-watched tribute videos in the history of YouTube. The Minecraft Java Edition launcher was updated to show a crowned pig in his honor. His battle against cancer — fought with humor, grace, and characteristic wit — inspired millions. The phrase "Technoblade Never Dies" became a permanent part of gaming culture.
Still Growing

In 2026, Minecraft continues to receive major updates. The game that Notch built in a weekend has outlasted entire game studios, platform generations, and cultural eras. 300 million copies sold. More than any game in history. A universe of biomes, mobs, mechanics, and stories — still expanding. Still surprising. Still endlessly creative. The final words of Minecraft's official in-game poem say it best: "And the game was over and the player woke up from the dream. And the player began a new dream. And the player dreamed again, endlessly."
